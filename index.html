Relationships among Angles, Arcs and Chords

1. Equal Angles and Equal Arcs (Theorem 10)

The Core Concept: If you slice a pie from the exact centre using identical angles, the curved crust pieces you create will be exactly the same length. It works perfectly in reverse, too!

Property Definition

Equal angles at the centre stand on equal arcs, and conversely, equal arcs subtend equal angles at the centre.

Condition 1: If \(\angle AOB = \angle COD\), then arc \(AB\) = arc \(CD\).

Reason: equal angles, equal arcs

Condition 2: If arc \(AB\) = arc \(CD\), then \(\angle AOB = \angle COD\).

Reason: equal arcs, equal angles

Variation: Angles at the Circumference

Because the angle at the centre is always twice the angle at the circumference, this rule applies to the edge too! Equal angles at the circumference also stand on equal arcs.

Self-Check Quiz

True or False: The theorem only works if the two equal angles are located inside the exact same circle; it cannot be applied to two separate circles.
False! The rule works in the same circle, OR in equal/congruent circles (circles that have the exact same radius).
Multiple Choice: In a circle with centre \(O\), arc \(PQ\) has a length of \(8\text{ cm}\) and subtends an angle of \(50^\circ\) at the centre. If another arc \(RS\) in the same circle also subtends an angle of \(50^\circ\) at the centre, what is the length of arc \(RS\)?
Explanation: (B) Because the angles at the centre are equal (\(50^\circ = 50^\circ\)), the arcs must be equal in length. Therefore, arc \(RS = 8\text{ cm}\).

2. Equal Angles and Equal Chords (Theorem 11)

Geometric Link: The arc is the curved crust of the slice, but the chord is the straight line connecting the slice's corners. If the central angles are identical, the straight chords must also be identical!

Property Definition

Equal angles at the centre stand on equal chords, and conversely, equal chords subtend equal angles at the centre.

Condition 1: If \(\angle AOB = \angle COD\), then chord \(AB\) = chord \(CD\).

Reason: equal angles, equal chords

Condition 2: If chord \(AB\) = chord \(CD\), then \(\angle AOB = \angle COD\).

Reason: equal chords, equal angles

Variation: Proof via Congruence

Why is this true? Because triangles \(\triangle AOB\) and \(\triangle COD\) are congruent! They share two equal radii (Side, Side) and an identical central angle (Angle). By SAS, the third sides (the chords) must be equal.

Self-Check Quiz

True or False: If two chords in a circle are equal in length, the triangles formed by connecting their endpoints to the centre will always have the exact same area.
True! Because equal chords subtend equal angles at the centre, the two triangles formed (\(\triangle AOB\) and \(\triangle COD\)) are perfectly congruent (SSS or SAS). Congruent triangles always have equal area.
Multiple Choice: Chords \(AB\) and \(CD\) are equal in length. \(\angle AOB = 60^\circ\). What kind of triangle is \(\triangle AOB\)?
Explanation: (B) \(\triangle AOB\) is an isosceles triangle because \(OA = OB\) (radii). If the central angle is \(60^\circ\), the base angles must be \((180^\circ - 60^\circ) / 2 = 60^\circ\). A triangle with three \(60^\circ\) angles is equilateral!

3. Equal Chords and Equal Arcs (Theorem 12)

The Bridge: We established that Angles = Arcs, and Angles = Chords. Logic dictates that the middleman can be removed: Equal Chords must directly equal Equal Arcs!

Property Definition

Equal chords cut off equal arcs, and conversely, equal arcs cut off equal chords.

Condition 1: If chord \(AB\) = chord \(CD\), then arc \(AB\) = arc \(CD\).

Reason: equal chords, equal arcs

Condition 2: If arc \(AB\) = arc \(CD\), then chord \(AB\) = chord \(CD\).

Reason: equal arcs, equal chords

Variation: The Logical Triangle

The textbook notes a golden rule: "If any one of 'equal angles', 'equal arcs', and 'equal chords' is true, the other two must be true." They are locked in a permanent, reversible triad of geometry.

Equal Arcs
↕
Equal Angles
↕
Equal Chords

Self-Check Quiz

True or False: This theorem implies that the straight length of a chord is exactly equal to the curved length of its arc.
False! A straight line is always the shortest distance between two points. The chord length is always strictly less than the arc length. The theorem only says that if Chord A = Chord B, then Arc A = Arc B.
Multiple Choice: In a regular hexagon inscribed perfectly inside a circle, what can you say about the six minor arcs cut off by the sides of the hexagon?
Explanation: (A) A regular hexagon has 6 sides of identical length. Because these sides act as chords inside the circle, and equal chords cut off equal arcs, all 6 arcs must be exactly equal in length.

4. Arcs Proportional to Angles (Theorems 13 & 14)

The Scaling Rule: If you double the angle of your pie slice, the crust edge of that slice becomes exactly twice as long! Arcs scale perfectly with their angles.

Property Definition

The ratio of the lengths of two arcs is equal to the ratio of the angles subtended at the centre (or at the circumference).

Condition: \(\text{Arc } AB : \text{Arc } CD = m : n\)

Reason (Centre): arcs prop. to \(\angle\)s at centre

Reason (Circumference): arcs prop. to \(\angle\)s at circumference

Conceptual Trap: Proportional Chords?

Do chords scale proportionately like arcs do? NO!
If you double an angle, the chord does NOT double. For example, a \(180^\circ\) chord (a diameter) is length \(2r\). A \(90^\circ\) chord is length \(\sqrt{2}r \approx 1.41r\).
\(2 \times 1.41r = 2.82r\), which is vastly larger than \(2r\). Chords do not scale linearly!

Self-Check Quiz

True or False: If arc \(AB\) subtends an angle of \(30^\circ\) at the circumference, and arc \(CD\) subtends an angle of \(90^\circ\) at the circumference, then arc \(CD\) is three times longer than arc \(AB\).
True! According to Theorem 14, arcs are directly proportional to the angles they subtend at the circumference. Since \(90^\circ / 30^\circ = 3\), the arc length ratio is also \(3:1\).
Multiple Choice: The ratio of the lengths of three arcs \(AB : BC : CA\) on a circle is \(1 : 2 : 3\). What is the size of the angle subtended by arc \(BC\) at the centre of the circle?
Explanation: (C) The total circle is \(360^\circ\). The angles at the centre will follow the arc ratio \(1 : 2 : 3\). Let the angles be \(x, 2x, 3x\). Their sum is \(6x = 360^\circ\), so \(x = 60^\circ\). The angle for arc \(BC\) is \(2x = 120^\circ\).
